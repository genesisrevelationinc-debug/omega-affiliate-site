# How To Do Keyword Research Free

Keyword research is a crucial step in search engine optimization (SEO). It helps you understand what people are searching for, which topics are trending, and where you can outrank your competitors. ## Understand Your Target Audience

Before diving into keyword research, it's essential to understand who your target audience is. What are their pain points? What questions do they ask when searching for solutions? This information will help you identify relevant keywords and phrases that resonate with them.

### Identify User Intent

User intent refers to the purpose behind a search query. Are users looking to learn something, buy a product, or solve a problem? By understanding user intent, you can tailor your content to match their expectations.

## Free Keyword Research Tools

While paid tools like Semrush offer advanced features, there are several free keyword research tools that can help you get started:

### Google Keyword Planner

Google's Keyword Planner is an excellent starting point for keyword research. This tool allows you to:

* Identify relevant keywords and phrases
* Estimate search volume and competition
* Get ideas for long-tail keywords

To access the Keyword Planner, follow these steps:

1. Sign in to your Google Ads account (if you don't have one, create a free account).
2. Navigate to Tools > Keyword Planner.
3. Enter your seed keyword or phrase.
4. Click on "Get ideas."

### Ahrefs' Free Keyword Research Tool

Ahrefs offers a limited version of their keyword research tool for free. This tool allows you to:

* Identify keywords and phrases
* Analyze search volume and competition
* Get suggestions for related keywords

To access the tool, visit [Ahrefs](https://ahrefs.com/) and click on "Free Keyword Research Tool."

## Manual Keyword Research Techniques

While online tools can help with keyword research, manual techniques are essential to validate your findings. Here's how:

### Brainstorming Sessions

Gather your team or conduct individual brainstorming sessions to generate a list of potential keywords and phrases.

* Write down all ideas, no matter how silly they may seem.
* Refine your list by filtering out irrelevant terms.

### Competitor Analysis

Analyze your competitors' websites to identify gaps in their keyword strategy. Look for:

* Uncovered long-tail keywords
* Opportunities for improvement
* Strategies that have worked for them (and can work for you)
