# How To Do Keyword Research Free: A Step-by-Step Guide

As a digital marketer, you're likely aware of the importance of keyword research in driving traffic and boosting conversions. However, many entrepreneurs and small business owners may not have the budget for expensive SEO tools or expertise. The good news is that you can do keyword research free with the right strategies and resources.

In this article, we'll walk you through a step-by-step guide on how to conduct free keyword research, including tools and techniques to help you get started.

## Understanding Keyword Research Basics

Before diving into the free tools and methods, let's quickly cover some keyword research fundamentals:

* **Keyword**: A word or phrase that users type into search engines to find relevant content.
* **Long-tail keywords**: More specific phrases with lower competition and higher conversion rates.
* **Search volume**: The number of searches for a particular keyword in a given time period.

Understanding these basics will help you create effective keyword research strategies, even on a limited budget.

## Free Keyword Research Tools

While paid tools like SEMrush (try it free here: https://www.semrush.com/lp/) offer advanced features and data, there are many free alternatives that can get you started:

* **Google Keyword Planner**: A popular tool for finding keywords, estimating search volume, and getting ideas.
* **Google Trends**: Analyze trending topics and see what's currently popular in your niche.
* **Answer the Public**: Use this tool to discover long-tail keyword opportunities and content ideas.

## How to Use Google Keyword Planner

Here's a step-by-step guide on using Google Keyword Planner for free:

1. Sign in with your Google account or create one.
2. Navigate to the "Tools" menu and select "Keyword Planner."
3. Enter your seed keywords and set filters (e.g., location, language).
4. Review the suggested keywords, including search volume and competition.

## How to Use Answer the Public

This tool helps you uncover long-tail keyword opportunities and content ideas:

1. Type in your main keyword or phrase.
2. Browse through the "Questions," "Prepositions," and "Compare" sections for inspiration.
3. Identify relevant phrases with lower competition and higher search volume.

## Leveraging Google Trends

To find trending topics and popular keywords, use Google Trends:

1. Enter a keyword or phrase in the search bar.
2. Analyze the results, including related topics and searches.
3. Use this data to inform your content strategy and target high-demand keywords.

## The Pros and Cons of Free Keyword Research Tools

Here's a comparison table highlighting the advantages and disadvantages of free tools:

| Tool | Pros | Cons |
| --- | --- | --- |

| Answer the Public | Uncover long-tail keywords, content ideas | No search volume or competition data |
